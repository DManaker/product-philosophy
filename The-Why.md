# Why did I get into Product Management?
My path into product management wasn’t traditional.

I started out wanting to be a physical therapist, drawn to diagnosing problems, creating a plan, and guiding people from pain to progress. That instinct never left.

When I pivoted into business, I worked across enterprise and mid‑market companies in customer‑facing roles like sales and marketing. There, I learned something foundational: **great ideas fail when they don’t meet real human needs.**

I’ve sold products that sounded compelling but missed the mark. That experience pushed me toward product management, the place where customer reality, business constraints, and execution collide. 

I believe product is a responsibility, not a feature factory. It shapes company trajectory, customer trust, and long‑term outcomes. 

At the heart of every problem is a product opportunity, but only if you’re willing to understand the human need beneath it. 

Over time, I’ve distilled my approach into a set of guiding principles. They aren’t theoretical. They’re forged from real customers, real tradeoffs, and real consequences. 

They serve as my compass for building products that create value for users and for the business.
